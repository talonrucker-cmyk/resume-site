Protecting the income you haven't earned yet.

Most of your wealth isn't in your accounts — it's in the paychecks still ahead of you. This lesson covers how the industry sizes that number, and how much of it you can actually insure.

Concept

Insurance replaces a paycheck, not a lifestyle.

  • 1A carrier will only insure an economic loss they can measure. If you die, the measurable loss is the income your family stops receiving.
  • 2A 25-year-old has 40 more years of paychecks ahead. A 62-year-old has three. Same salary, very different loss.
  • 3So underwriters cap coverage as a multiple of income — and that multiple shrinks as you age.
  • 4Past retirement age the math flips to net worth, because there's no paycheck left to replace — only an estate to settle.
You can't buy $10M of coverage on a $60k salary. The cap isn't the carrier being difficult — it's the carrier refusing to insure a loss that doesn't exist.

The one exception

Debt is insurable on top of the income multiple. A mortgage, business loan, or co-signed obligation is a real dollar figure your family inherits — so carriers will add it to the cap.

How to read it

Find the age band, multiply by gross annual income, then add outstanding debt. That's the ceiling — not a recommendation. What you actually need is usually less.

Green vs. amber

Green bands are priced off income. Amber bands are priced off net worth — the switch happens at retirement, when the paycheck stops being the thing at risk.

Concept

Your largest asset doesn't show up on a statement.

  • 1Economists call it human capital — the present and future value of every paycheck between now and retirement.
  • 2Early in a career it dwarfs everything else. A 25-year-old earning $80k has a portfolio of roughly $8M in future wages and maybe $10k in the bank.
  • 3Raises compound. A steady 4% annual increase roughly doubles your salary every 18 years — so late-career years carry the most weight.
  • 4Which is why the qualification cap almost always lands well below lifetime earnings. Coverage replaces the loss; it doesn't reproduce the career.
You insure your car, your house, and your phone — and leave the eight-figure asset uninsured because it doesn't come with a statement.

06 — HUMAN CAPITAL CALCULATORCAP USES CURRENT-AGE MULTIPLE
Concept

The more likely event is that you survive it.

  • 1A working adult is far more likely to miss a long stretch of work from illness or injury than to die during their career.
  • 2Disability insurance replaces a slice of monthly income — typically 60% of base pay, often capped in dollars.
  • 3Group coverage through work usually excludes bonus and commission, so high earners are covered on a fraction of what they actually make.
  • 4Whatever the policy doesn't replace is the gap — the monthly shortfall your savings absorbs until you're back.
Life insurance protects your family from your death. Disability insurance protects them from your life.
