02 — THE WEALTH-BUILDING ACCOUNTCLICK THE TABS
Concept

Every raise you fully spend is a raise you never keep.

  • 1Lifestyle creep is spending rising to match income. The bigger paycheck arrives and the bigger life quietly arrives with it.
  • 2Feel it or not, it caps you: a career of raises can end with the same savings rate as your first job — zero.
  • 3The fix isn't sacrifice. It's letting spending grow a little slower than income and investing the gap.
  • 4The 1% test: hold lifestyle growth one point under your raises. You'll never feel the difference — the market will.
You don't have to spend less than today. You just can't spend all of tomorrow's raise.

Where the gap comes from

Income grows 4% a year. Hold spending to 3% and the shaded wedge — the wealth-building room — widens every year.

04 — THE 1% TESTADJUST ANY FIELD — RESULTS UPDATE LIVE
Concept

Before you invest a dollar, collect the free ones.

  • 1The 401(k) match is your employer paying you to save. A common "50% up to 6%" means every dollar you put in — up to 6% of pay — earns 50 cents on top, instantly.
  • 2Not contributing enough to get the full match is a guaranteed raise, declined. No investment beats an immediate 50% return.
  • 3Benefits are pay too: an HSA, cheaper group insurance, an ESPP discount, tuition help. Read the packet — the money is already budgeted for you.
  • 4Watch the vesting schedule. Match dollars may not be fully yours until you've stayed a few years.

The match, in numbers

On a $80,000 salary, contributing 6%:

$4,800you contribute (6%)
+$2,400employer adds (50%)

That's $2,400 a year you'd walk past by not enrolling — before a cent of growth.

Free money first. Then your own.
05 — THE MATCH & WORK BENEFITSCHECK YOUR BENEFITS PORTAL
Concept

A rollover just moves the account — it doesn't cash it out.

Old 401(k)left at a former job
→
Rollover IRAsame money, your control
  • 1When you leave a job, the 401(k) stays behind. A rollover moves that balance into an IRA — or your new employer's plan — tax-free.
  • 2Done right (a direct rollover, custodian to custodian) you never touch the money, so there's no tax and no penalty.
  • 3Why bother? One account instead of four, usually lower fees, and far more investment choices than a workplace menu.

Keep it "direct"

Ask for a direct or trustee-to-trustee transfer. If the old plan mails you a check instead, the clock starts: miss the 60-day window and the IRS treats it as a withdrawal.

Match the tax type

Roll traditional into traditional, Roth into Roth. Mixing them can trigger a tax bill you didn't plan for.
