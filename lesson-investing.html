The analogy

Pick your lane by when you exit, not how you feel.

◆ HOV laneA financial planner beside you
Co-pilotReads the road ahead
Far-left lanesRetirement · 10+ years
Full speedHighest risk tolerance
Middle lanesMedium goals · ~5 years
SteadyBe ready to exit on time
Right lanesShort-term · 1–3 years
Slow & congestedCars entering & exiting

The right lanes crawl because everyone's merging on and off — that's short-term money, where safety beats speed. The far left runs fast because no one's exiting soon. Put money in the wrong lane and a crash forces you to exit at the worst possible moment.

Why full speed

With a decade or more before you exit, short-term crashes have time to recover. Volatility is the price of the market's highest long-run returns — and time is what makes it affordable.

The mix is a starting point, not a prescription — the HOV lane helps you tune it.
03 — MATCH YOUR MONEY TO A LANEILLUSTRATIVE MIXES — NOT ADVICE
Concept

A crash slows every lane — and you can't schedule it.

  • 1Downturns are uncontrollable and inevitable. Nobody knows when — only that another one is coming, the way traffic eventually jams.
  • 2The crash hits all lanes at once. What differs is who's forced to exit in it — and short-term money is riding right next to the off-ramp.
  • 3Money with years left simply waits out the jam. The road always cleared before; the whole market has recovered from every crash in history.
  • 4So the danger isn't the crash — it's needing to sell during one. Lane choice decides whether you have to.
04 — THE CRASHMARKETS HAVE RECOVERED FROM EVERY DOWNTURN
Concept

Everyone agrees: buy low, sell high. Then feelings flip it.

  • 1At the top, everything feels great and safe — so people pile in. That's euphoria, and it's exactly where prices are highest.
  • 2At the bottom, everything feels terrifying — so people sell to make the pain stop. That's panic, and it's exactly where prices are lowest.
  • 3Follow the feeling and you buy high and sell low — the precise opposite of the rule everyone claims to believe.
  • 4The fix is a plan you set before the emotion: keep investing on schedule, and let the far-left lane ignore the noise.

06 — TWO INVESTORS, ONE CRASHSAME MARKET — DIFFERENT DECISIONS
Recap

Three rules of the road

01 — Choose the lane

Match risk to time

Money you need soon rides the slow right lane; money you won't touch for a decade runs full speed on the left. The horizon picks the lane, not your mood.

Time = lane
02 — Expect the crash

Don't exit in traffic

A downturn is inevitable and hits every lane. If your money has years left, you wait it out — the road has always cleared.

Wait, don't sell
03 — Beat the instinct

Buy low, sell high

Fear says sell at the bottom; greed says buy at the top. A plan set in advance — and an HOV-lane co-pilot — keeps feelings from driving.

Plan > panic
